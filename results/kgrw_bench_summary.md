## Cross-dataset verdict: tail-fraction predicts KGRW advantage

Connecting tail-fraction (from `scripts/tail_fraction.py`) to matched-density CKA:

| Dataset | |T|/|V_L| @ k=4 | mean r_L(w) | KGRW low-budget dCKA | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DBLP APAPA  | 0.44 | 14.0 | **+0.080** | Balanced tail; KGRW Phase-1 captures 44% exactly at k=4 ‚Üí wins narrowly |
| ACM PAPAP   | 0.18 | 54.3 | **‚àí0.023** | Hub-heavy; KMV Phase-1 undersamples 82% of endpoints ‚Üí KGRW loses |
| IMDB MDMDM  | 0.63 | 4.8  | **+0.037** | Tail-dominated; KMV captures 63% exactly ‚Üí KGRW wins |

**Theoretical mechanism**: By Pointwise Exactness, K_‚Ñì[w] exactly reconstructs N^{-1}(w) iff r_‚Ñì(w) ‚â§ k. So |T|/|V_L| @ k is the fraction of endpoints KMV captures without sampling loss. KGRW's advantage scales with tail fraction ‚Äî and loses on hub-heavy graphs where MPRW's walks find high-degree nodes naturally.

## Empirical verdict for supervisor

1. **Coupon-collector in MPRW is real** ‚Äî clean monotone decay visible on DBLP and IMDB marginal-cost curves.
2. **Tail-fraction predicts KGRW wins**: high |T|/|V_L| ‚Üí KGRW advantage (DBLP, IMDB); low ‚Üí MPRW wins (ACM).
3. **KGRW is NOT a framework-level win**: advantage is narrow (only lowest budget, +0.04 to +0.08 CKA) and requires tail-dominated structure.
4. **Not publishable as a standalone algorithm.** The theory (Pointwise Exactness + Saturation-Triggered Walks, THEORY.md ¬ß10) is sound, but the empirical regime where it matters is too narrow to be a contribution.
5. **Best story remaining**: DBLP APTPA IS-bias narrow regime (L=4 meta-path, starved budget) ‚Äî already documented in `project_dblp_aptpa_is_regime.md`.
