\begin{abstract}
Heterogeneous Graph Neural Network (HGNN) inference on meta-path-induced relational graphs $H_\mathcal{P}$ has an architectural ceiling: $H_\mathcal{P}$ is $O(|V|^2)$ in the worst case. On production-scale knowledge graphs --- OGB~MAG ($736$\,K target nodes, $2.86$--$13.63$\,B relational edges across its three densest meta-paths) --- exact materialization completes in $17$--$34$ minutes, but the subsequent GNN forward pass is killed by out-of-memory at every depth $L\in\{2,3,4\}$. This paper presents \textbf{KMV graph reconstruction}, a systems-level workaround.

We extend the KMV sketch propagation framework~\cite{berahmand2024kmv} to emit a sparse approximate adjacency $\widetilde{A}$ of size $O(|V|\cdot k)$. Each hash retained in a terminal sketch points back to its origin node via the global hash map maintained at initialization; resolving these pointers reuses the propagation pass already required for centrality estimation at zero extra computation. On the full OGB~MAG graph, KMV at $k{=}8$ completes both materialization (under $10$\,s) and inference ($\sim$$1.2$\,s) where Exact cannot run inference at all (Table~\ref{tab:scalability}).

At medium scale (ACM, DBLP, IMDB, PubMed; full materialization feasible), KMV and Meta-Path Random Walks (MPRW)~\cite{dong2017metapath2vec} achieve statistically indistinguishable Macro-F1 at matched output density. MPRW retains a $1$--$9$\,pp Linear CKA advantage; we observe a coincident structural difference (MPRW's walk-completion sampling retains $3$--$8$\,pp more homophilic edges than KMV's hash-based sampling on ACM and DBLP) but do not run the importance-corrected MPRW baseline that would establish a causal mechanism. Two finite-sample bounds---a Spatial Variance Bound on per-layer aggregation and a Bounded Representation Drift on multi-layer drift---characterise KMV's intermediate behavior; neither bound implies a guarantee on downstream classification loss.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Knowledge graphs (KGs) encode multi-typed entities and relationships as heterogeneous networks $\mathcal{G} = (\mathcal{V}, \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{L})$.
A meta-path $\mathcal{P} = (x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_L)$ specifies a sequence of node types, and the \emph{relational graph} $H_\mathcal{P} = (V, E)$ contains an edge $(u, v)$ for every pair of target-type nodes connected by at least one meta-path instance.
Heterogeneous GNNs such as HAN~\cite{wang2019han}, MAGNN~\cite{fu2020magnn}, and SeHGNN~\cite{yang2023sehgnn} require the explicit adjacency matrices of these relational graphs.

The bottleneck is \emph{materialization}: constructing $H_\mathcal{P}$ costs $O(|V| \cdot |\mathcal{E}^*|)$ time and $O(|V|^2)$ space.
On production-scale KGs---OGB~MAG with 1.13M papers and 2.86B relational edges, OAG~CS with 1.9M papers and 16.6B edges---exact materialization triggers out-of-memory failures or exceeds hour-long timeouts.
Scalable GNN techniques like GraphSAGE~\cite{hamilton2017sage}, SIGN~\cite{frasca2020sign}, and GAMLP~\cite{zhang2022gamlp} reduce training cost but still require the full adjacency matrix for pre-computation.
Stochastic sampling via Meta-Path Random Walks (MPRW)~\cite{dong2017metapath2vec} avoids full materialization but offers no analytical density control and yields importance-sampled (hub-biased) neighborhoods.

\textbf{This paper} extends KMV sketch propagation~\cite{berahmand2024kmv}---originally designed for approximate hub-query centrality on relational graphs---to act as a graph sparsifier for HGNN inference, addressing the materialization bottleneck above.
Each hash retained in a terminal KMV sketch carries a pointer to its originating node via a global hash map maintained at initialization; resolving these pointers reuses the propagation pass already required for centrality estimation and produces a sparse approximate adjacency $\widetilde{A}$ of size $O(|V| \cdot k)$ at zero extra computation.

\textbf{Main empirical result.} On the full OGB~MAG graph ($736$\,K papers, $2.86$--$13.63$\,B edges across the three densest meta-paths), Exact materializes $H_\mathcal{P}$ in $17$--$34$ minutes, but the subsequent GNN forward pass is killed by OOM at every depth $L \in \{2,3,4\}$. KMV at $k{=}8$ completes both materialization and inference in under $\sim$$10$\,s and $\sim$$1.2$\,s respectively (Table~\ref{tab:scalability}). This regime---where the binding constraint is downstream HGNN inference on the materialized adjacency, rather than propagation itself---is the operating point for which KMV reconstruction is intended.

\textbf{Scope of claims.} On medium-scale benchmarks where exact materialization is feasible, KMV does not outperform Meta-Path Random Walks (MPRW)~\cite{dong2017metapath2vec} on Macro-F1; the two methods are statistically interchangeable at matched output density. We document two extensions we attempted to obtain a structural KMV-vs-MPRW gap on node classification (a hybrid scheme, Appendix~\ref{sec:app-kgrw}; and a narrow regime where MPRW's importance-sampling bias measurably degrades F1, Appendix~\ref{sec:app-dblp-aptpa}); each is reported with its operating range.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{KMV Graph Reconstruction} (Algorithm~\ref{alg:reconstruction}): a simple modification to the existing centrality-oriented sketch propagation that emits a sparse approximate adjacency $\widetilde{A}$ of size $O(|V| \cdot k)$ at zero additional propagation cost.
\item \textbf{Scaling demonstration on full OGB~MAG.} Exact OOMs on inference; KMV completes in seconds (Table~\ref{tab:scalability}).
\item \textbf{Two finite-sample bounds} on the propagation: Spatial Variance Bound (Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial}) on per-layer aggregation, Bounded Representation Drift (Theorem~\ref{thm:drift}) on multi-layer drift. Neither implies a guarantee on classification loss; their scope is stated explicitly at the top of \S\ref{sec:theory}.
\item \textbf{Medium-scale evaluation.} KMV ties MPRW on Macro-F1 within seed noise on DBLP, IMDB, and PubMed; ACM is reported as a saturated meta-path control. The Linear CKA gap that favours MPRW is described, not claimed as a causal mechanism.
\item \textbf{Structural preservation profile.} The bottom-$k$ index $\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ preserves cardinality, expansion-decay rate (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i4}), and Jaccard \emph{ranking} (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i5}); it does \emph{not} preserve absolute Jaccard at intermediate $L$, nor the hub-favoured edge subset that MPRW concentrates on.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{Knowledge Graphs and Relational Graphs}

A \emph{knowledge graph} $\mathcal{G} = (\mathcal{V}, \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{L})$ consists of typed nodes and typed edges.
A \emph{meta-path} $\mathcal{P} = (x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_L)$ is a sequence of $L+1$ node types.
A meta-path \emph{instance} is an ordered sequence $(u_0, u_1, \ldots, u_L)$ where each $u_i$ has type $x_i$ and consecutive nodes are connected by an edge of the required type.
The \emph{relational graph} $H_\mathcal{P} = (V, E)$ has $V = \mathcal{V}_0$ (all nodes of type $x_0$) and $(u, v) \in E$ iff at least one meta-path instance connects them.

\subsection{KMV Sketch Propagation}

The K-Minimum Values (KMV) sketch~\cite{cohen1997size} estimates set cardinality by retaining the $k$ smallest uniformly random hash values.
Given a set $C$, assign each element $o$ a hash $r(o) \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)$, and define $\mathcal{K}(C) = \{k \text{ smallest } r(o) : o \in C\}$.
The cardinality estimator is $|\widetilde{C}| = (k-1) / \max \mathcal{K}(C)$.

The base paper~\cite{berahmand2024kmv} introduces \emph{sketch propagation}: initialize each target-type node with its own hash, then propagate sketches along meta-path edges, merging at each level by retaining the $k$ smallest values.
Terminal sketches enable hub-query centrality estimation (degree, h-index) with $>90\%$ F1 and 10--191$\times$ speedup over exact methods.

\subsection{Related Work}

\textbf{HGNNs.}
HAN~\cite{wang2019han}, MAGNN~\cite{fu2020magnn}, SeHGNN~\cite{yang2023sehgnn}, and HeCo~\cite{wang2021heco} all assume pre-materialized adjacency matrices.

\textbf{Scalable GNNs.}
GraphSAGE~\cite{hamilton2017sage}, SIGN~\cite{frasca2020sign}, and GAMLP~\cite{zhang2022gamlp} reduce training cost but still require the full adjacency for pre-computation.

\textbf{Stochastic sampling.}
MPRW / Metapath2Vec~\cite{dong2017metapath2vec} avoids full materialization via random walks but provides importance-sampled (hub-biased) neighborhoods with no analytical density control.

\textbf{Gap.}
No existing method provides memory-bounded approximate adjacency with formal guarantees for HGNN inference on unmaterialized relational graphs.

\section{KMV Graph Reconstruction}
\label{sec:reconstruction}

\subsection{Algorithm}

The key observation is that a global hash map $\mathcal{H}: r(u) \to u$ registered at initialization allows each terminal sketch hash to be resolved to its originating node in $O(1)$.
This converts the statistical KMV data structure into a topological one at zero extra propagation cost.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{KMV Graph Reconstruction}
\label{alg:reconstruction}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE KG $\mathcal{G}$, meta-path $\mathcal{P}$, sketch size $k$
\ENSURE Sparse approximate adjacency $\Atilde$
\STATE $\Atilde \gets \mathbf{0}$; $\mathcal{H} \gets \emptyset$
\FOR{$u \in \mathcal{V}_0$}
  \STATE $r(u) \gets \text{Rand}(0,1)$
  \STATE $\mathcal{H}[r(u)] \gets u$; $\mathcal{K}[u] \gets \{r(u)\}$
\ENDFOR
\STATE \textsc{Propagate}($\mathcal{K}$) along $\mathcal{P}$ \hfill // base sketch propagation~\cite{berahmand2024kmv}
\FOR{$v \in \mathcal{V}_L$}
  \STATE $u_v \gets$ node in $\mathcal{V}_0$ corresponding to $v$
  \FOR{$x \in \mathcal{K}[\bar{v}]$}
    \STATE $u_x \gets \mathcal{H}[x]$ \hfill \COMMENT{$O(1)$ lookup}
    \STATE $\Atilde[u_x, u_v] \gets 1$; $\Atilde[u_v, u_x] \gets 1$
  \ENDFOR
\ENDFOR
\RETURN $\Atilde$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Complexity}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Propagation}: $O(|\mathcal{E}^*| \cdot k)$ --- each edge traversed once per sketch entry.
\item \textbf{Reconstruction}: $O(|V| \cdot k)$ hash lookups.
\item \textbf{Space}: $O(|V| \cdot k)$ for sketch arrays and output $\Atilde$.
\end{itemize}

Compared to exact materialization ($O(|V|^2)$ space), KMV reconstruction reduces memory by a factor of $|V|/k$.
For ACM ($|V| = 3{,}025$, $k = 32$): exact adjacency has 9.15M edges; KMV produces 96.8K edges --- a 94.5$\times$ reduction.

\subsection{Inference Complexity}

Exact GNN message-passing requires $O(L \cdot |V|^2 \cdot d^2)$ per layer (dense $A$).
With KMV's sparse $\Atilde$ ($\leq |V| \cdot k$ non-zeros), inference cost becomes $O(L \cdot |V| \cdot k \cdot d^2)$ --- converting the quadratic bottleneck to linear.

\section{Theoretical Analysis}
\label{sec:theory}

\textbf{Scope of the bounds in this section.}
Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial} bounds the per-layer drift of KMV's feature aggregation $\tilde{\mu}_u$ against the exact aggregation $\mu_u$, in $\ell_\infty$ on a single coordinate. Theorem~\ref{thm:drift} composes per-layer drift through $L$ Lipschitz layers to bound the representation drift $\|\tilde{h}_u^{(L)} - h_u^{(L)}\|_2$. \emph{Neither bound implies a guarantee on classification loss or on $\arg\max$ agreement with the true label $y$.} A bounded representation drift can still flip a $\arg\max$ at the readout layer, and conversely a representation can change substantially without changing $\arg\max$ on the held-out test set. We therefore use the bounds as predictors of \emph{intermediate} faithfulness (depth-wise CKA, Pred.\ Agreement against an Exact-trained reference embedding), not of downstream task performance, which we measure separately and empirically in \S\ref{sec:experiments}.

\subsection{Spatial Variance Bound}

\begin{lemma}[Spatial Variance Bound]
\label{lem:spatial}
Let $x_v \in \mathbb{R}^d$ with $\|x_v\|_\infty \leq \mathcal{M}$.
Let $\tilde{\mu}_{u,j}$ and $\mu_{u,j}$ be the KMV-approximated and exact feature aggregation for dimension $j$.
If $|\mathcal{N}(u)| > k$, then by Bernstein's inequality for sampling without replacement:
\[
  \Pr\!\left(|\tilde{\mu}_{u,j} - \mu_{u,j}| \geq t\right)
  \leq 2 \exp\!\left(- \frac{k\,t^2}{2\sigma_j^2 + \tfrac{2}{3}\mathcal{M}\,t}\right)
\]
where $\sigma_j^2 = \mathrm{Var}(x_{v,j})$ over $v \in \mathcal{N}(u)$.
\end{lemma}

Key properties: (i)~$\mathbb{E}[\tilde{\mu}_u] = \mu_u$ (unbiased), (ii)~error decays as $O(1/\sqrt{k})$, (iii)~\textbf{independent of $|V|$ and $\Lambda$ (max degree)}.
When $|\mathcal{N}(u)| \leq k$, the full neighborhood is retained exactly.

\subsection{Bounded Representation Drift}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded Representation Drift]
\label{thm:drift}
Let an HGNN apply weight $W^{(\ell)}$ and $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz activation $\sigma$ at each layer $\ell = 1, \ldots, L$.
Define $C^{(\ell)} = L_\sigma \cdot \|W^{(\ell)}\|_2$ and $C = \max_\ell C^{(\ell)}$.
Then the representation drift at depth $L$ satisfies:
\[
  \|\tilde{h}_u^{(L)} - h_u^{(L)}\|_2 \leq \varepsilon_k \cdot L \cdot C^L
\]
where $\varepsilon_k = O\!\left(\sqrt{d \log(d/\delta) / k}\right)$ with probability $\geq 1-\delta$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
At a single layer: $\|\tilde{h}_u - h_u\|_2 \leq L_\sigma \cdot \|W\|_2 \cdot \|\tilde{\mu}_u - \mu_u\|_2$ by Lipschitz continuity.
Applying Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial} with threshold $t = \sqrt{(c/k)\log(2d/\delta)}$ and a union bound over $d$ dimensions:
$\|\tilde{\mu}_u - \mu_u\|_2 \leq \varepsilon_k$.
Multi-layer drift decomposes recursively:
$\Delta^{(\ell+1)} \leq C^{(\ell)} \cdot \Delta^{(\ell)} + C^{(\ell)} \cdot \varepsilon_k$.
Unrolling from $\Delta^{(0)} = 0$ yields the result.
\end{proof}

The bound decays with $k$ (more sketch budget $\to$ tighter approximation) but grows with $L$ (deeper networks compound per-layer error) --- the \emph{budget--depth trade-off} measured empirically in Section~\ref{sec:experiments}.

\section{Experimental Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

\sloppy

\subsection{Two-Stage Inductive Protocol}
\label{sec:methodology-protocol}

Training a graph encoder directly on a stochastic sparsifier's output $\widetilde{A}$ exposes the gradient to edge-set noise that varies between runs and between epochs, destabilising optimisation. We therefore adopt a two-stage inductive protocol that decouples the sparsifier from training. Stage~I uses the exact adjacency to fit encoder weights; Stage~II uses the sparsifier at inference only, with frozen weights.

\textbf{Stage I (training).} Partition the target-type node set $\mathcal{V}_0$ into a training subset $V_\text{train}$ ($|V_\text{train}|/|\mathcal{V}_0|=0.4$) and a test subset $V_\text{test}$ (the remaining $0.6$). Where the underlying KG carries temporal metadata on the target type --- e.g., paper publication year for DBLP and OGB~MAG --- the partition is chronological, with $V_\text{train}$ containing the oldest $40\%$. Otherwise the partition is uniform at random with seed $42$. Materialise the exact relational graph $A_\text{train}$, restricted to the subgraph induced by $V_\text{train}$ (edges $(u,v)$ where both $u,v\in V_\text{train}$). Train a GraphSAGE encoder $f_\theta$ with mean aggregation, hidden dimension $64$, on $(A_\text{train}, X_{V_\text{train}})$ to convergence: cross-entropy loss, Adam optimiser, early stopping with patience $30$, maximum $200$ epochs. Save the frozen weights $\theta^*$. We repeat this for each model depth $L \in \{1,2,3,4\}$ separately, producing four checkpoints per dataset.

\textbf{Stage II (inference).} For each method under test, apply the corresponding materialiser to the \emph{full} graph (i.e.\ to $\mathcal{V}_0$, not to $V_\text{train}$) to obtain a target adjacency $\widetilde{A}$. Compute $Z = f_{\theta^*}(\widetilde{A}, X)$ followed by the linear classifier head co-trained in Stage~I. All metrics are computed on $V_\text{test}$ only --- nodes the encoder has not seen edges incident to during training.

\subsection{Baselines}
\label{sec:methodology-baselines}

We compare three materialisers, each invoked through a uniform child-process interface for measurement parity (\S\ref{sec:methodology-measurement}).

\emph{Exact.} The exact relational graph $A = H_\mathcal{P}$, computed by the C++ implementation released with the base paper~\cite{berahmand2024kmv}, which we use unmodified. When Exact is used at inference, $Z_\text{exact}$ is the reference embedding against which the two approximators are measured.

\emph{KMV.} Algorithm~\ref{alg:reconstruction}, implemented in C++ as an additional command on top of the same propagation engine used for Exact. The output $\widetilde{A}_\text{KMV}$ has at most $|V|\cdot k$ entries.

\emph{MPRW (Meta-Path Random Walks)~\cite{dong2017metapath2vec}.} For each $u\in\mathcal{V}_0$, launch $w$ independent random walks following meta-path $\mathcal{P}$. The retained adjacency $\widetilde{A}_\text{MPRW}$ is the union of (source,~terminal) pairs over all completed walks. MPRW runs as a $w$-sweep with each $w$ a separate materialisation; we do not perform density-matching calibration because the resulting density is part of the comparison axis (\S\ref{sec:methodology-grid}). MPRW is implemented as a native C++ binary using the same graph-loading and timing infrastructure as Exact and KMV; this avoids interpreter overhead in the memory measurement.

\subsection{Parameter Grid}
\label{sec:methodology-grid}

The empirical grid producing the figures of \S\ref{sec:experiments} and the data of Table~\ref{tab:fidelity} sweeps three parameters:
\begin{align*}
k &\in \{2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64\} && \text{(KMV sketch size)} \\
w &\in \{1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512\} && \text{(MPRW walk budget)} \\
L &\in \{1, 2, 3, 4\} && \text{(model depth)}
\end{align*}
KMV $k$ and MPRW $w$ both range over consecutive powers of two; the $w$-range extends one decade beyond the $k$-range so MPRW reaches densities matched to KMV at the larger $k$ values.

\noindent\textbf{Replicates.}
For every $(k, L, \text{dataset})$ cell we run $50$ KMV replicates with independent hash seeds. For every $(w, L, \text{dataset})$ cell we run $10$ MPRW replicates with independent walk-RNG seeds (eight on IMDB). Exact is deterministic; we record one run per $(L, \text{dataset})$ cell. Cell-level standard deviations reported in Table~\ref{tab:fidelity} are computed across the replicates of that cell. Cross-dataset comparisons fix $k{=}32$, $w{=}32$, $L{=}2$ as the operating point unless stated otherwise.

\subsection{Measurement Protocol}
\label{sec:methodology-measurement}

We require comparable timing and memory measurements across Exact, KMV, and MPRW. All three materialisers are invoked as child processes under a uniform external wrapper that records peak resident memory and wall-clock time, ensuring that the reported metrics come from a single, instrument-agnostic source.

\emph{Peak resident memory} is read from the wrapper's report. \emph{Algorithm wall-clock} is read from a timing line emitted by the C++ implementation itself; the timer is started \emph{after} the typed graph $G^*$ is loaded into memory and stopped before output is written. All three implementations emit this line at the same point relative to graph load, so the reported time excludes graph load uniformly. \emph{Inference} is measured by the same wrapper: the frozen $\theta^*$ checkpoint is loaded, the materialiser's output is fed through, and inference time and peak memory are recorded. An entry of \emph{INF-FAIL} in Table~\ref{tab:scalability} denotes a process killed by the operating system during inference for exceeding available memory; the materialisation step in such rows completed normally.

\subsection{Fidelity Metrics}
\label{sec:methodology-metrics}

Every metric below is computed on $V_\text{test}$ only.

\emph{Macro-F1.} Standard multi-class macro-averaged F1 of the linear classifier head's predictions against the ground-truth labels of $V_\text{test}$. Reported as the mean over replicates with cell-level standard deviation.

\emph{Linear CKA}~\cite{kornblith2019cka}.
For two embedding matrices $Z_\text{exact}, Z_\text{approx}\in\mathbb{R}^{|V_\text{test}|\times d}$,
$\mathrm{CKA}(Z_\text{exact}, Z_\text{approx}) = \frac{\|Z_\text{approx}^\top Z_\text{exact}\|_F^2}{\|Z_\text{exact}^\top Z_\text{exact}\|_F \cdot \|Z_\text{approx}^\top Z_\text{approx}\|_F}$
(centered features). CKA is invariant to orthogonal transforms and isotropic scaling --- the standard representation-similarity metric for comparing sparsifier outputs to a reference encoder.

\emph{Prediction Agreement (PA).} The fraction of $V_\text{test}$ on which $\arg\max Z_\text{exact}(u) = \arg\max Z_\text{approx}(u)$ at the linear classifier head. This is the $0/1$ classification analogue of CKA --- a direct measure of how often the approximate model would make the same decision as the exact one.

\emph{Depth-wise CKA.} For an $L$-layer encoder, the per-layer Linear CKA between exact and approximate intermediate representations $h^{(\ell)}_\text{exact}$ and $h^{(\ell)}_\text{approx}$ at each $\ell\in\{1,\ldots,L\}$. Used to localise where representational drift accrues with depth.

\emph{Dirichlet Energy.} For the encoder output $h\in\mathbb{R}^{|V|\times d}$, the standard graph-signal Dirichlet energy
$\mathcal{E}(h) = \sum_{(u,v)\in E} \|h_u-h_v\|_2^2$
computed against the exact adjacency $E = E(A_\text{exact})$. A diagnostic for over-smoothing collapse as depth increases.

We use Macro-F1 against the ground-truth label as the headline downstream metric and Linear CKA / PA against $Z_\text{exact}$ as the headline fidelity metrics. The drift bounds of \S\ref{sec:theory} apply to intermediate aggregations and are therefore most directly tested by depth-wise CKA, not by Macro-F1.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{KMV vs MPRW: Operational Comparison}
\label{sec:discussion-comparison}

The experimental evidence shows that KMV and MPRW deliver \emph{comparable} downstream quality.
Neither method dominates across all datasets: KMV leads on DBLP APTPA at $L{=}4$ with starved budgets (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-dblp-aptpa}), MPRW leads on ACM PTP at $L{=}2$ (the saturated, hub-dense regime), and the two are statistically interchangeable on IMDB and PubMed.

The contribution of KMV is therefore not a quality advantage over MPRW but a set of operational properties as an approximation of Exact:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Space.} $O(|V| \cdot k)$ output, in contrast to Exact's $O(|V|^2)$.
\item \textbf{Density control.} A single analytical parameter $k$ controls the output edge count; no calibration sweep is required.
\item \textbf{Determinism.} A single deterministic propagation pass; no seed variance budget.
\item \textbf{Finite-sample bounds.} Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial} and Theorem~\ref{thm:drift} characterise per-layer and multi-layer aggregation drift, in contrast to MPRW's asymptotic convergence in $w$.
\end{itemize}

MPRW's higher CKA ($0.973$--$0.996$ vs.\ $0.848$--$0.977$) reflects its importance-sampled walk structure preserving the hub geometry of the exact graph (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-mprw-bias}). However, this alignment is concentrated in a class-agnostic hub subspace, and higher CKA does not translate to higher F1 on three of the four medium-scale datasets (Table~\ref{tab:fidelity}).

\subsection{What $\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ Preserves}
\label{sec:discussion-preservation}

The interchangeability of KMV and MPRW for the SAGE-mean classifier is a property of the downstream task, not a deficiency of either sparsifier.
Mean-aggregating GraphSAGE on a sparsified adjacency depends only on the \emph{cardinality} of each retained neighborhood, not on \emph{which} neighbors are retained: at matched cardinality, the two sparsifiers are interchangeable substrates.
The structural information the bottom-$k$ index $\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ does carry---each retained hash points back to a specific origin node via the global hash map $\mathcal{H}$, so any two nodes' neighborhoods are not just sized but \emph{identifiable}, and hence comparable---is unused under mean aggregation.

The experiments in this paper supply a partial profile of which structural properties of the exact $L$-hop neighborhood the index $\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ preserves and which it loses.
On the preservation side: \emph{cardinality} (the original purpose of the sketch~\cite{berahmand2024kmv}); \emph{per-node expansion-decay rate}, with $\log\max\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ slope correlating with $\log|N_L(v)|$ slope at Pearson $r{=}-0.997$ on PubMed, $-0.992$ on IMDB, and $-0.789$ on DBLP (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i4}); \emph{bottom-$k$ Jaccard ranking} between $L$-hop neighborhoods at Pearson $r{>}0.93$ across $L\in\{1,\ldots,4\}$ on three datasets (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i5}); and---against an MPRW baseline at matched density---a $2$--$6\times$ higher spectral radius $\lambda_{\max}$, evidence that KMV preserves expander structure where MPRW collapses to a walk skeleton (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-mprw-bias}).
On the loss side: \emph{absolute Jaccard at intermediate $L$} is biased downward by up to $10$\,pp---the ranking is preserved but absolute calibration is not (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i5}); the \emph{hub-favoured edge subset} on which MPRW concentrates is not retained, which is a disadvantage for KMV on homophilic classification tasks where the discriminative structure lives in the hub subgraph (the ACM saturation case in Table~\ref{tab:fidelity} is the extreme); and \emph{$\arg\max$ agreement on downstream classification} is not implied by the drift bounds of \S\ref{sec:theory}, which apply to intermediate aggregations only.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

We list the threats to the claims of this paper explicitly.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Bounds do not cover classification loss.} Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial} bounds the per-layer aggregation drift, and Theorem~\ref{thm:drift} bounds the representation drift. Neither bounds the classification loss or the agreement with the true label $y$.
\item \textbf{The MPRW baseline is uncorrected importance sampling.} An importance-reweighted MPRW baseline~\cite{frasca2020sign,zhang2022gamlp} that removes the homophily bias measured in \S\ref{sec:homophily} is not included in this evaluation; the residual MPRW-vs-KMV CKA gap may shrink under such a correction.
\item \textbf{The HGNN architecture used is GraphSAGE, not Simple-HGN.} Our quality numbers are GraphSAGE Macro-F1; HGB benchmark results suggest tuned simple architectures can establish a stronger ceiling.
\item \textbf{Saturation regimes are degenerate.} ACM PTP at $\rho{=}1.00$ (\S\ref{sec:fidelity-orig}) and ACM I4 (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i4}) reflect a saturated meta-path where the relational graph is a near-clique; the fidelity numbers and the slope correlation respectively become uninformative there.
\item \textbf{Memory advantage is at the output, not the working set.} On the C++ binary used for measurement, KMV and Exact have comparable in-process RAM at the medium-scale benchmarks; the savings appear in the output adjacency size and at the inference stage on graphs where the output dominates.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Tighten Theorem~\ref{thm:drift}.} Empirically the propagation is tight: no information is lost in the bottom-$k$-of-bottom-$k$ merge across the four datasets and $L\in\{1,\ldots,4\}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:app-i2}). The current exponential-in-$L$ bound is therefore loose by construction. A topology-aware tighter bound conditioning on per-node max-intermediate-degree is open.
\item \textbf{Importance-corrected MPRW.} A reweighted MPRW baseline (\S\ref{sec:homophily}) is the clean test of whether the homophily structure of MPRW's retained edges is causally responsible for the CKA gap.
\item \textbf{Motif and spectral preservation.} The bottom-$k$ representation preserves \emph{set} information but not \emph{multiset}. Whether $L$-hop motif counts and spectral statistics survive the propagation is open, and would identify which classes of downstream task can consume $\mathcal{K}_L$ directly.
\item \textbf{Sketch-aware training.} End-to-end training on $\widetilde{A}_\text{KMV}$ with variance reduction (control variates, antithetic sampling), removing the requirement that an Exact training subgraph be feasible at all.
\item \textbf{Streaming and temporal updates.} KMV propagation is monotone (per-level $\min$ only decreases); sketches admit incremental updates as edges arrive. Quantifying the regret of incremental KMV against periodic re-propagation is open.
\item \textbf{Stronger HGNN backbone.} The $1$\,pp KMV/MPRW/Exact tie at medium scale is reported under GraphSAGE with mean aggregation. A tuned Simple-HGN-style architecture would establish a stronger Macro-F1 ceiling and is the natural next quality benchmark.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presents KMV graph reconstruction (Algorithm~\ref{alg:reconstruction}), a systems-level workaround for the $O(|V|^2)$ ceiling of exact relational-graph materialization in HGNN inference.
The main empirical result is that on the full OGB~MAG graph (three densest meta-paths, $2.86$--$13.63$\,B edges) Exact's GNN forward pass is killed by OOM at every depth, while KMV at $k{=}8$ completes both materialization and inference in seconds (Table~\ref{tab:scalability}).
Two finite-sample bounds (Lemma~\ref{lem:spatial}, Theorem~\ref{thm:drift}) characterise KMV's intermediate-feature behavior; their scope is stated explicitly in \S\ref{sec:theory} and does not extend to classification loss.

At medium scale, KMV and MPRW are statistically interchangeable on Macro-F1 at matched density (within $\sim$1\,pp on DBLP, IMDB, and PubMed; ACM is reported as a saturation control).
MPRW retains a Linear CKA advantage which we describe but do not establish as causal; the importance-corrected MPRW baseline that would test the homophily-bias hypothesis is listed as open work (\S\ref{sec:future}).
The mechanistic question of which structural properties of $H_\mathcal{P}$ the bottom-$k$ index $\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ preserves---cardinality, expansion-decay rate, and rank-Jaccard yes; absolute Jaccard and the hub-favoured edge subset no---is the program we hand to follow-on work.

\section{Expansion-Decay Signal}
\label{sec:app-i4}

Beyond average aggregation, the KMV sketch carries an additional signal: the rate at which $\log\max\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ shrinks with depth $L$.
Under a per-node expansion model $|N_L(v)| \approx |N_{L-1}(v)| \cdot \beta_v$, the expectation of $\max\mathcal{K}_L[v]$ scales as $k/|N_L(v)|$, giving the relation
\[
  \mathrm{slope}_L\bigl(\log\max\mathcal{K}_L[v]\bigr) \;=\; -\,\mathrm{slope}_L\bigl(\log|N_L(v)|\bigr).

\section{Hybrid Scheme: KGRW}
\label{sec:app-kgrw}

We hypothesised that KMV's deterministic Phase~1 propagation could remove MPRW's two known inefficiencies---dead walks at sparse meta-paths, and coupon-collector saturation at dense ones---by giving each walk a known set of source endpoints to start from. We implemented this as KGRW: KMV propagation for $\lfloor L/2\rfloor$ hops, walks for the remaining $\lceil L/2\rceil$, edges generated by joining sketch entries to walk endpoints.

After replacing a flawed round-robin pairing with cross-product joining, KGRW achieves $+0.080$ CKA over MPRW on DBLP APAPA at the lowest budget ($k{=}4$, $w'{=}1$, $\sim$2.5\,K edges) and $+0.037$ on IMDB MDMDM. On ACM PAPAP it loses ($-0.023$), and at moderate budgets on every dataset the advantage vanishes (Figs.~\ref{fig:kgrw-depth}--\ref{fig:kgrw-tail}). The advantage tracks a node-level \emph{tail-fraction} statistic ($|T|/|V_L|$ at each $k$) that the practitioner cannot easily pre-compute and that varies across dataset/meta-path pairs. The regime in which KGRW wins is too narrow to support a standalone algorithm.

\section{Homophily Bias and Spectral-Radius Inversion}
\label{sec:app-mprw-bias}
\label{sec:homophily}

Table~\ref{tab:fidelity} shows MPRW's Linear CKA at $0.93$--$1.00$ versus KMV's $0.89$--$0.98$---a consistent $1$--$9$\,pp gap that does not translate into a Macro-F1 advantage. We probe one candidate mechanism: \emph{edge-level label homophily}.

\paragraph{Definition.}
For an undirected sparsified adjacency $\widetilde{A}$ and a class label $y_u$ assigned to each target node $u$,
\[
  \mathrm{homophily}(\widetilde{A}) \;=\; \frac{|\{(u,v)\in\widetilde{A}\,:\,y_u = y_v\}|}{|\{(u,v)\in\widetilde{A}\,:\,y_u, y_v \text{ both labelled}\}|},
\]
i.e.\ the fraction of retained edges whose two endpoints carry the same class label, restricted to edges where both endpoints are labelled. ACM and DBLP have full label coverage on their target node sets. PubMed has $1.83\%$ label coverage and is statistically underpowered for this measurement; we exclude it. We did not run this measurement on IMDB.

\paragraph{Measurement.}
For each dataset we materialise $\widetilde{A}_\text{KMV}$ at $k\in\{4,8,16\}$ and $\widetilde{A}_\text{MPRW}$ at the closest-edge-count $w$, then compute homophily on the retained edge set. Reference Exact-graph homophily is $0.523$ on ACM (saturated, near-clique) and $0.465$ on DBLP. Both sparsifiers retain a \emph{more} homophilic subset than Exact (sparsification preferentially keeps within-class edges); the question is whether the two sparsifiers retain it at the same level.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
On ACM and DBLP, at three matched-density levels each, MPRW's retained edges have a $3.3$--$7.9$\,pp higher fraction of same-class endpoints than KMV's. This is a descriptive observation about the structure of the retained edge sets; nothing in the measurement establishes that this homophily difference \emph{causes} the CKA gap of \S\ref{sec:fidelity-orig}. A plausible structural mechanism is that walks completing $L$ meta-path hops preferentially traverse well-connected intermediate nodes, and well-connected nodes link same-class endpoints more often than cross-class ones in these datasets; we do not test this hypothesis here. A clean causal test is an importance-reweighted MPRW baseline at matched density (\S\ref{sec:future}).

\paragraph{Caveats.}
The measurement covers two datasets. The Macro-F1 numbers in Table~\ref{tab:fidelity}, where KMV and MPRW tie within seed noise, remain the more robust comparison; the CKA gap is suggestive of a structural asymmetry, not a fidelity verdict.
